Classification of Countries

To determine whether a country is Developed, Developing, and Least Developed, the United Nations uses a metric called the Human Development Index (HDI). HDI was created to highlight the country’s achievement in its social and economic forms.

As we can see, in the Bar graph, the classification that has the highest number is the Developing. We can interpret that most countries still have yet to reach their full potential of improving their citizen’s lives. It is followed by developed and least developing.

In the Pie graph, almost half of the 52 countries with 48.1% are Developing. Followed by developed with 32.7%, and then least developing comes in last with 19.2%.
